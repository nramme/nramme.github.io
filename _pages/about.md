Welcome! I am a geneticist who uses whole-genome data to understand how parasite populations evolve, adapt, and spread. My research combines population genomics, bioinformatics, and statistical genetics to investigate population structure, relatedness, and signatures of natural selection. I am particularly interested in transforming complex genomic datasets into meaningful biological insights that can inform infectious disease surveillance, control, and elimination strategies.

I obtained my PhD in Genetics and Molecular Biology from the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul (UFRGS). I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Parasitology at the University of São Paulo (USP). During my training, I was a visiting scholar at the Institute for Genome Sciences at the University of Maryland. My current research focuses on the genomics and evolutionary history of malaria parasites, with particular emphasis on Plasmodium vivax, zoonotic malaria, and host-switching events involving Plasmodium simium.
